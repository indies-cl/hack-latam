# hack@latam

let's code for a better world.

# who we are

indies.la is a collective of 1.5k+ latin american builders.  
we sit at the intersection of:

- indie hackers
- YC-backed founders
- non-profits and open-source

# track record

- 1.5k+ members across latam
- monthly events with around 75 in-person attendees
- companies in the community have shipped real products and raised capital
- strong overlap with top-tier ecosystems (y combinator, platanus, chilean startup scene)

indies.la is where ambitious latam builders already hang out.

# the event

hack@latam. May 15–17, 2026, 54 hours, fully focused on social impact.

- 150 participants, teams of 1–4 people
- fully remote, coordinated via discord in spanish
- optional in-person kick offs in chile, peru, and guatemala
- target demographic: ages 20–35 — software engineers, cs students, founders, high-agency techies
- application-based selection to filter for people who actually ship

goal: turn latent talent into founders building for latin america.

# what makes this different

- deployed software only
- clear tracks tied to real problems
- judges with domain expertise
- follow-ups with teams that show traction

most hackathons optimize for photos, not outcomes. we seek real change.

# themes & tracks

we focus on three fault lines in latam.  
each track is about data, coordination, and real-time action.

1. transparency & corruption

problem: opacity kills trust and progress.  
angle: use data to expose, track, and reduce corruption.

2. environment & climate risk

problem: water, contamination, fires, and climate risk.  
angle: track, predict, and mitigate environmental damage.

3. defensive acceleration

problem: large-scale threats like cyberattacks, disinformation, and critical infrastructure failures.  
angle: build and accelerate technologies that strengthen society's defenses.

# format & judging

- 54-hour build sprint
- teams form around tracks and local pain points
- deployed MVP required for judging (live demo, not slides)

judging criteria:

- real problem, clearly targeted
- working product, not just mockups
- path to continue post-hackathon
- impact potential in latin american context

each track judged by practitioners in the field.  
we are already in conversations with civic tech, non-profit, and policy leaders to sit on the jury.

# why this matters

latin america is full of unsolved problems:

- fires in the south, floods and droughts across the region
- weak infrastructure, fragile institutions, and opacity
- communities solving things locally with almost no tools

we are not importing silicon valley's problems.  
we are building for latam's reality.

hack@latam is a forcing function: put top local talent together, ship code on real problems.
